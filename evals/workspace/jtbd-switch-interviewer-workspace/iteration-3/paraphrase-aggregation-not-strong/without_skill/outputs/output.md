# JTBD Switch Interview Synthesis: Word to Notion

## Team Hypothesis Under Test
"Users switch from Microsoft Word to Notion because they want collaboration."

---

## 1. Per-Interview Synthesis (Verbatim vs. Paraphrase)

### Interview I1
- **Evidence type:** Paraphrase only. No verbatim quote captured.
- **Researcher summary:** User switched for collaboration features; easier doc sharing with team.
- **What we actually have:** Researcher's interpretation that the switch driver was "collaboration." We do not know the user's own framing, the trigger event, the prior struggle moment, or the specific job being hired.
- **Caveats:** Researcher used the team's hypothesis word ("collaboration") directly -- possible echo of the team's prior.

### Interview I2
- **Evidence type:** Paraphrase only. No verbatim quote captured.
- **Researcher summary:** Collaboration came up "several times"; real-time editing with team called the main reason.
- **What we actually have:** Frequency claim ("several times") and a sharper sub-feature ("real-time editing") -- but again as researcher restatement, not user words. "Main reason" is a researcher judgment, not a user attribution.
- **Caveats:** "Main reason" framing is exactly the kind of phrasing that gets inserted by a researcher carrying a hypothesis into the conversation.

### Interview I3
- **Evidence type:** Paraphrase only. No verbatim quote captured.
- **Researcher summary:** User wanted to work with others on the same document; collaboration central to decision.
- **What we actually have:** Same construct ("collaboration") with no user-language anchor. No old-solution friction, no new-solution moment of relief, no context of use.
- **Caveats:** Three different researchers converging on the exact same abstract noun ("collaboration") is itself a signal of shared framing, not necessarily of shared user reality.

---

## 2. Cluster Strength Assessment

**Hypothesis:** "Switch from Word to Notion is driven by collaboration."

**Verbatim quote count supporting cluster:** 0
**Paraphrase count supporting cluster:** 3
**Independent researchers:** 3 (good on independence dimension)
**JTBD STRONG bar:** 3+ independent **verbatim** quotes capturing the user's own language about the switch trigger, old-solution struggle, or new-solution hire.

**Verdict: NOT STRONG.**

Reasoning:
- Paraphrase aggregation does not upgrade to STRONG. Three researchers writing "collaboration" tells us three researchers heard something they classified as collaboration -- it does not tell us three users used that frame, named that trigger, or described the same job.
- Researcher-bias amplification is the dominant risk here. The team carried a "collaboration" hypothesis in; three notes came back saying "collaboration." That convergence is consistent with confirmation effects, not just with signal.
- The construct itself is too abstract to be a JTBD finding. "Collaboration" could mean real-time co-editing, async commenting, share-link permissioning, visibility into who edited what, handoff from writer to reviewer, or simply "I don't want to email .docx files anymore." These are different jobs with different competitive sets.
- We have no struggle moments, no first-thought-of-switch moments, no anxieties about the new tool, no habits the new tool had to overcome. The classic switch-interview spine is missing.

**Strength label: WEAK / DIRECTIONAL.** Worth following up on, not worth shipping decisions on.

---

## 3. Recommended Next Action

1. **Do not promote the hypothesis to a validated insight.** Do not let it drive roadmap or positioning yet.
2. **Re-interview with verbatim capture.** Run 5 to 8 new switch interviews with recordings or transcripts. Require verbatim quotes for any claim that ends up in synthesis.
3. **Use a hypothesis-neutral protocol.** Anchor on the switch timeline (first thought, passive looking, active looking, deciding event, first use, new habit) rather than asking about "collaboration." Let the user name the job.
4. **Decompose "collaboration" before the next round.** Pre-write the three or four sub-jobs it might actually be (real-time co-edit, comment/review handoff, shareable link, visibility/audit, async doc-as-workspace) so interviewers can hear which one the user actually describes -- and notice when none of them fit.
5. **Audit interviewer framing.** Review I1 to I3 audio or notes for leading questions. If the word "collaboration" was introduced by the researcher first, that is a known contamination pattern and those three data points should be downgraded further.
6. **Set an explicit STRONG bar for the next round.** 3+ independent verbatim quotes that name the same trigger moment in the user's own words, sourced from interviews where the researcher did not introduce the construct first.

**Bottom line:** Three paraphrases pointing at the same abstract noun is a hypothesis worth testing, not a hypothesis that has been tested.
