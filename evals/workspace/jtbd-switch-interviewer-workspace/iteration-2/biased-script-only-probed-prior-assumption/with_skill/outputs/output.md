## 3. Script bias assessment (load-bearing section)

**Question:** Did the script probe all four forces (PUSH, PULL, ANXIETY, HABIT), or only PUSH + PULL framed around the team's prior assumption?

**Answer:** Only PUSH and PULL were probed, and both were framed exclusively around **speed**. The script is a single-axis confirmation instrument.

| Force | Probed? | Framed neutrally? | Evidence in script |
|---|---|---|---|
| **PUSH** (what made Notion intolerable) | Yes, but only on the speed axis | **No** -- Q3 presupposes Notion has a "slowest thing" that "bothered" the subject | Q3, Q5 |
| **PULL** (what made Obsidian attractive) | Yes, but only on the speed axis | **No** -- Q2 frames the comparison as "in terms of speed" | Q2, Q5 |
| **ANXIETY** (what made switching scary: data migration, plugin reliability, sync risk, lock-in fears) | **No** | n/a | None |
| **HABIT** (what kept them on Notion: team has it, templates built, shared workspaces, muscle memory) | **No** | n/a | None |
| **TRIGGER event** (what concrete moment started the switch search) | **No** | n/a | Q1 opens the door, then abandons it |
| **STRUGGLE window** (alternatives evaluated, criteria used) | **No** | n/a | None |

Additional script defects, per persona Step 2 / Step 4:

1. **Q2 and Q3 are presuppositional.** They embed the team's hypothesis as a premise. A clean JTBD script asks "walk me through Notion in the weeks before you started looking for something else" and lets latency surface (or not) on its own.
2. **Q4 is a textbook leading question.** "Are there *other* reasons *besides speed*?" -- asked *after* Q2 and Q3 have primed speed -- is a confirmation-bias accelerant, not a neutral probe. By this point the subject has been told three times that speed is the topic of interest. Cooperative subjects round to the frame; only an unusually self-anchored subject (I3) escapes it, and even I3 capitulates with "but mostly speed when you ask."
3. **Q5 is a yes/no leading close.** "Would you say speed is the main reason?" is the kind of question used to **close a sale**, not to discover a job-to-be-done. It should never appear in a JTBD script.
4. **No timeline reconstruction.** The persona's Step 2 requires walking the subject backward through SWITCH -> STRUGGLE -> TRIGGER. None of that structure exists here.
5. **No four-forces coverage check before fieldwork.** A JTBD script must have at least one neutral probe per force before any interview is run.

**Conclusion:** The "users want speed" pattern observed across all three interviews is **an artifact of the instrument**, not a finding. The script measured itself.

Per persona Quality Bar: the run is failing if motivation assumptions survive without confirmation, disconfirmation, or an honest gap entry. This section IS the honest gap entry. **Concrete next step (single recommendation):**

Re-run the interview round with a corrected script before any motivation decision is locked. The corrected script must:

1. Open with timeline anchoring: *"Walk me through the moment you decided to try Obsidian. What happened that day or that week?"* -- and stay on the timeline until the subject names a concrete TRIGGER event.
2. Cover all four forces with at least one neutral probe each, **before** any axis-specific follow-up:
   - PUSH: *"In the weeks before you started looking, what about your Notion setup was wearing on you?"* (no presupposed axis)
   - PULL: *"When you first tried Obsidian, what made you keep going past day one?"* (no presupposed axis)
   - ANXIETY: *"What worried you about leaving Notion? What almost stopped you?"*
   - HABIT: *"What's still on Notion for you, and why hasn't it moved?"*
3. Forbid yes/no closes ("would you say speed is the main reason?"). Replace with ranking questions only *after* forces have been independently surfaced: *"You mentioned A, B, and C -- if you had to rank them, which one would have stopped the switch if it weren't true?"*
4. Capture subject context (role, team vs solo, Notion tenure, what they tried before Obsidian).
5. Target n >= 5 fresh subjects so that any cluster reaching 3+ is reaching it on its own merits, not via instrument echo.
